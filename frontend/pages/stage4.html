Exhaust

Burned gases are expelled from the cylinder

About This Stage
During the exhaust stroke, the exhaust valve opens while the intake valve remains closed. The piston moves upward, pushing the burned gases out of the cylinder through the open exhaust valve. This process clears the cylinder of combustion byproducts, preparing it for the next cycle. The upward motion of the piston reduces the cylinder's volume, forcing the exhaust gases out into the exhaust system. Once complete, the cycle returns to the intake stroke, and the process repeats continuously.
