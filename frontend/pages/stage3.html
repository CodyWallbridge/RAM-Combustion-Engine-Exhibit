Power

Combustion drives the piston downward

About This Stage
The power stroke begins when the spark plug ignites the compressed air-fuel mixture, causing a controlled explosion. Both valves remain closed during this process. The rapid expansion of the burning gases forces the piston downward with tremendous force, converting the chemical energy from the fuel into mechanical energy. This downward motion is what drives the crankshaft and ultimately powers the vehicle. The power stroke is the only stroke that produces work in the four-stroke cycle.
